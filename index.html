Regardless of freight type or asset model, modern trucking companies grapple with systemic issues that impact profitability and efficiency.

Full Truckload (FTL) Specific Hurdles

While FTL offers direct transport, its challenges revolve around maximizing efficiency per load and minimizing unproductive travel.

  • üõ£Ô∏è High Deadhead Miles:Suboptimal route planning leads to 5-8% higher deadhead miles, directly impacting fuel costs and driver productivity.

  • üîÑ Finding Return Loads:Inefficient processes for securing backhaul opportunities lead to empty truck miles and lost revenue.

Less-Than-Truckload (LTL) Specific Hurdles

LTL's inherent complexity stems from consolidating multiple shipments, leading to unique quoting, visibility, and operational issues.

  • ‚úçÔ∏è Complicated Quoting & Data Inaccuracy:Extensive data required leads to errors, with 15-65% of freight invoices containing errors, causing disputes and delayed payments.
  • üí≤ Hidden Accessorial Fees:Unexpected charges affect nearly 4 in 10 shipments, up from 1 in 10 pre-pandemic, leading to budget overruns.
  • üì¶ Unreliable Transit Times & Damage:Hub-and-spoke networks lead to delays (extending transit by a full day) and increased damage risk (1-2% higher than FTL) due to frequent handling.
  • üìú Upcoming NMFC Changes (July 2025):Shift to density-based classification will dramatically alter rates and require system overhauls to avoid costly errors.
